Why We Picked It

A single pump expands into a dense, visibly tinted foam that's easy to see where you've shaved — genuinely useful for avoiding missed patches, especially around the jawline. It provided noticeably more glide than typical drugstore aerosol foam in our testing, at almost the same price point, thanks to a formula built around niacinamide, sea kelp extract, and calming panthenol.

It comes in two main variants: a Cedar Wood scent (cedar plus orange peel) and a Fresh Scent/Sensitive Skin formula aimed at normal and dry skin. The Cedar Wood scent is the most common source of complaints — some reviewers describe it as "unconventional" or compare it to nail-salon products, finding it too strong. There are also occasional quality-control complaints about bottles that leak from the lid or don't dispense evenly, wasting product.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you want a noticeable step up from basic aerosol foam without paying premium-cream prices, and you like the visible-tint feature for tracking coverage.

Skip it if: you have reactive or fragrance-sensitive skin — this formula does contain fragrance, unlike the fragrance-free Proraso aftercare line. If pure lather quality on sensitive skin is your top priority, our #2 pick, Cremo Sensitive Skin Shave Cream, is fragrance-free.

Common Questions

Does GilletteLabs shave gel require a GilletteLabs razor?+
No — it's designed to pair with GilletteLabs' exfoliating-bar razors but works fine with any razor, including cartridge and safety razors.
Which scent should I pick?+
Cedar Wood has a stronger, more distinctive scent that some find polarizing; the Fresh Scent/Sensitive Skin variant is milder and aimed at normal-to-dry skin.
Is it fragrance-free?+
No — both variants contain fragrance. If you need a fragrance-free option, Cremo's Sensitive Skin Shave Cream is a better fit.
Why doesn't the foam dispense evenly sometimes?+
A number of reviewers report occasional bottle/pump quality-control issues causing leaking or uneven dispensing. This isn't universal, but it's a recurring complaint worth being aware of.
