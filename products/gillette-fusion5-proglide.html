Why We Picked It

If you want the single most reliable "just works" razor with zero setup, the Fusion5 ProGlide is it. Five tightly spaced blades give an ultra-close shave, and the FlexBall pivoting head lets the cartridge tilt independently to follow the contours of your jaw and neck — most testers needed a beat of extra pressure to actually feel it engage, but it does measurably reduce missed patches on curved areas. An enhanced lubrastrip runs both in front of and behind the blades, and a small precision trimmer on the back of the cartridge handles sideburns and mustache edges without switching tools.

It's the best-selling, most-reviewed cartridge razor on the market for a reason: this is the pick for guys who don't want to think about their razor at all. It also sits in the middle of Gillette's own lineup — a step up from the plain Fusion5 and Mach3, and one notch below the pricier GilletteLabs Exfoliating/Heated razors, which use an incompatible cartridge mount and add a mechanical exfoliating bar that some reviewers find more gimmick than upgrade.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you want the closest shave available at any drugstore or supermarket with no learning curve, no blade-angle technique to master, and refills you can buy anywhere in a pinch.

Skip it if: you shave daily and are trying to lower your long-term cost per shave — a safety razor like the Merkur 34C costs a fraction as much per shave once you're past the learning curve. It's also worth skipping if five blades tend to aggravate your skin; the Gillette SkinGuard or a safety razor are gentler options.

Common Questions

Do Fusion5 ProGlide cartridges fit GilletteLabs razors?+
No. GilletteLabs razors use a different cartridge mount to accommodate their built-in exfoliating bar, so ProGlide refills won't fit — and vice versa. Check the handle you own before buying refills.
How many shaves does one cartridge really last?+
Most reviewers report a noticeable drop-off in comfort and closeness after about 5–7 shaves, even though Gillette markets the blades as long-lasting. Rinsing thoroughly and drying the cartridge between uses extends this somewhat.
Is the FlexBall pivot actually noticeable while shaving?+
It's subtle. Several reviewers note you have to apply a bit of extra pressure on curves (like the jawline and neck) before you can feel the head tilt — it's a real feature, just not a dramatic one.
Is the Fusion5 ProGlide good for sensitive skin?+
It can cause irritation for reactive skin given the five-blade pass count. If razor burn is a recurring issue, the Gillette SkinGuard or a mild safety razor is usually a better starting point.
