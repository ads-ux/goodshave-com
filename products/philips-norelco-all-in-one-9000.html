Why We Picked It

This is the trimmer we recommend to guys who just want one drawer, not five. It ships with interchangeable heads for beard, stubble, ear/nose, and body, and its self-sharpening titanium/stainless blades held a guard length consistently across a full head of coarse beard growth in testing. Nearly six hours of runtime on a charge means it's genuinely a grab-and-go tool, not something you have to plan a charging schedule around.

Philips sells several attachment-count variants of this line — the base MG9510/60 ships with 21 pieces and 19 length settings, while newer SKUs (MG9575/49, MG9580/40) extend to 23–25 pieces and a wider 0.5–20mm range. The most common complaints are about build details rather than core performance: the precision guard has been called "thick and wonky" with a slight rattle, some SKUs don't include a charger or changed the USB port (breaking compatibility with old cables), and there's no travel lock, so accidental power-on in luggage is a real risk. Durability over the long haul is well-regarded, with an 8-year warranty and multiple users reporting 3+ year old units still performing well.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you want a single tool that covers beard trimming, body grooming, and detail work (ears/nose) without buying and storing separate devices.

Skip it if: pure beard-line precision is your top priority — the Wahl Lithium Ion line edges it out for dedicated face/beard trimming with a wider head and more guard intervals at a lower price, and comes with a longer 5-year warranty. Also consider the Bevel Pro if you specifically have coarse or curly facial hair prone to bumps.

Common Questions

How many attachments does it actually come with?+
It depends on the SKU: the base MG9510/60 ships with 21 pieces (19 length settings), while newer MG9575/49 and MG9580/40 kits include 23–25 pieces with a wider length range up to 20mm.
Is one all-in-one tool really as good as separate dedicated trimmers?+
For most guys, yes — it covers 90% of grooming needs well. If pure beard-line precision is your top priority, a dedicated trimmer like the Wahl Lithium Ion edges it out slightly.
Does it come with a charger?+
This varies by SKU and has changed across generations — some newer kits don't include one, and the USB port style has changed too, which can break compatibility with older cables. Check the specific listing before buying.
How durable is it long-term?+
Well-regarded — it carries an 8-year warranty, and multiple long-term reviewers report units still performing well after 3+ years of regular use.
