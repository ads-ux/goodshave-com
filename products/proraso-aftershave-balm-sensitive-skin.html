Why We Picked It

Proraso's sensitive-skin (White line) formula is built around green tea and oat extract rather than alcohol, and it noticeably reduced redness in our testers within minutes of application. It's an unusual ingredient mix by American drugstore standards, but the Italian shaving tradition it comes from has been refining this exact formula for decades — around 87% of its ingredients are natural-origin, and it skips parabens, phthalates, silicones, mineral oil, and SLS.

Proraso actually sells four distinct lines, and picking the wrong one is the most common point of confusion: White (this one) is formulated for sensitive skin with green tea and oat; Green is the classic refreshing eucalyptus/menthol formula for all skin types; Blue is a more protective anise/amber formula for dry skin; and Red is a richer, nourishing sandalwood formula aimed at coarser beards. All four were recently updated into a "Super Formula" version in solid-colored tubes, which most users describe as a modest improvement over the old glass-bottle formula.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you have sensitive or reactive skin and want an effective, inexpensive, alcohol-free aftershave with a mild scent that won't overpower cologne.

Skip it if: you want a stronger, more traditional barbershop scent and cooling sensation — Proraso's own Green line or our #3 pick, Cremo Cooling Post Shave Balm, deliver more of that menthol kick. Skip it too if you specifically want a splash-style aftershave with a stronger sting/scent tradition — Clubman Pinaud is the classic alternative.

Common Questions

Which Proraso line should I buy?+
White (Sensitive) is built for reactive skin with green tea and oat. Green is the classic menthol/eucalyptus all-skin-types formula. Blue targets dry skin with anise/amber. Red is richer and aimed at coarser beards. Pick based on your skin type first, scent preference second.
Why does the scent fade so quickly?+
Proraso's White and Blue lines use a deliberately subtle scent profile — reviewers note it fades within about an hour, faster than the more assertive Green or Red lines.
Is the new 'Super Formula' worth it over the old formula?+
Most reviewers describe it as a modest improvement, not a dramatic change — the classic glass-bottle formula is still being sold through in some markets.
Is Proraso good for daily use?+
Yes — it's alcohol-free and fast-absorbing, which makes it suitable for daily use without the drying effect of traditional splash-style aftershaves.
