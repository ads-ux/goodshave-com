Why We Picked It

If you want premium foil-shaver performance without buying into the Braun ecosystem, the Arc5's five-blade head delivers some of the closest results of any electric shaver we cover — reviewers consistently note it "plows through" thicker, coarser growth better than most competitors. The pop-up trimmer is genuinely useful for sideburns, and the motor holds up well over years of daily use.

One thing worth knowing before you buy: Panasonic sells essentially the same shaving head under several different model numbers — LV65, LV95, LV67, and LV97 — and per independent comparisons, the actual shaving performance is identical across the line. The only real differences are whether a cleaning/charging station is included and the style of the battery/time display. Don't pay extra for a different model number expecting a better shave — you're paying for the station. The newest Arc5 Pro line (launched May 2026) is a genuine step up, with a stronger 70,000 cross-cuts/minute motor and an adaptive beard-density sensor.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you have thicker or coarser facial hair and want maximum cutting power, and you don't mind a fluid-based cleaning station over Braun's cartridge system.

Skip it if: you want the gentlest possible shave on sensitive skin — the Braun Series 9 Pro+ is rated gentler by most reviewers, with a longer battery runtime and a more travel-friendly cleaning system.

Common Questions

What's the difference between the Arc5 LV65, LV95, LV67, and LV97?+
According to independent comparisons, the shaving performance is identical across all four — they differ only in whether a cleaning/charging station is included (LV95/LV97 have one, LV65/LV67 don't) and the style of the battery display.
Is the Arc5 or the Braun Series 9 better?+
It depends what you value: the Arc5 generally wins on raw cutting power for thick, coarse hair, while the Braun is rated gentler for sensitive skin and has a longer battery life. Many reviewers call it a genuine toss-up depending on your beard type.
Why do people say the Arc5's design has a flaw?+
The head's steeply sloped design has no grip surface, and some users report their hand slipping while tapping out loose hair — which can dent the foil, an expensive part to replace (~$72).
What's new with the 2026 Arc5 Pro?+
Panasonic launched the Arc5 Pro (ES-L550WS/ES-L580WS) in May 2026 with a diagonal finishing-foil pattern, a stronger 70,000 cross-cuts/minute motor, and an adaptive beard-density sensor — a real step up from the older LV-series models.
