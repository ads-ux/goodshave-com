Why We Picked It

The Rockwell 2C's flippable two-setting baseplate — position 1 on one side, position 3 on the other — makes it a genuinely gentler entry point into double-edge shaving than a fixed-aggressiveness razor like the Merkur 34C. Start on setting 1 while you're learning the correct blade angle, then flip the baseplate to setting 3 once you're comfortable. Blade costs are just as low as any safety razor since it takes standard double-edge blades, and Rockwell backs the razor with a lifetime warranty.

The tradeoff for the simplified 2-setting design is limited range: some experienced forum reviewers describe setting 1 as "so mild it barely feels like there's a blade in there," and the common advice is that paying roughly $20 more for Rockwell's own 6C (6 settings) gets you meaningfully more versatility if you think you'll want to dial in a more precise aggressiveness later. The zinc-alloy head and handle (rather than stainless steel, which Rockwell reserves for its 6S/T2 models) has also drawn some durability questions over years of use, though it's not a common failure point.

Who It's For — and Who Should Skip It

Get this if: you want to try safety razor shaving but are nervous about jumping straight into a fixed-aggressiveness razor, or you have sensitive skin and want to start as mild as possible with room to step up.

Skip it if: you're confident you want more precise control over aggressiveness from day one — Rockwell's own 6C offers 6 settings for a modest price increase. Skip it too if you'd rather have a proven, simple fixed razor; the Merkur 34C is the classic alternative.

Common Questions

Is setting 1 on the Rockwell 2C too mild to be useful?+
Some experienced reviewers find setting 1 extremely mild — useful mainly while you're first learning blade angle. Most people move to setting 3 within the first few weeks.
Should I get the Rockwell 2C or the 6C?+
If you want more control over aggressiveness as your technique improves, the 6C's six settings (vs. the 2C's two) are usually worth the roughly $20 price difference. The 2C is fine if you mainly want a simple, gentle starting point.
Is the zinc-alloy construction a problem?+
It's not a common failure point, but it's a step down from the stainless steel used in Rockwell's pricier 6S and T2 models. Most users don't report durability issues within normal use.
What blades come included, and are they good?+
The 2C ships with 5 Swedish stainless double-edge blades, which are a reasonable starting point — most wet-shavers eventually experiment with a sampler pack to find their preferred blade.
